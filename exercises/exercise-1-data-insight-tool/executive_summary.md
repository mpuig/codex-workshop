## Executive Summary

We evaluated 68 regions across Spain, Portugal, Italy, and France using a composite opportunity index. The index combines market penetration data, demographic indicators, and risk exposure metrics. **Top opportunities are concentrated in large Italian and French urban regions where MIG's market share is below 0,3% and demand indicators are strong.** If MIG reaches 2% share in the top five regions, estimated incremental gross written premium (GWP) is approximately EUR 582.041.602.

---

## Methodology

We evaluated all 68 regions using a weighted scoring model with six factors:

| Factor | Weight | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Market share gap (inverse) | 30% | Lower current share = more room to grow |
| Accident frequency | 20% | Higher claims frequency signals demand for coverage |
| Population size | 15% | Larger markets offer greater scale potential |
| GDP per capita | 15% | Higher income = greater ability to purchase insurance |
| Population growth | 10% | Growing populations expand the addressable market |
| Natural disaster risk | 10% | Higher risk drives demand for property and health cover |

Each factor was normalized to a 0-100 scale and combined into a single Opportunity Score (max 100).

## Key Takeaways

1. **Italy and France represent the largest untapped opportunity.** MIG's average market share in Italy is ~0,35% and in France ~0,14%, compared to ~6,7% in Spain and ~5,5% in Portugal.

2. **The addressable market in the Top 5 regions alone is substantial.** If MIG reached just 2% market share across these five regions, the estimated incremental GWP would be approximately EUR 582.041.602.

3. **Prioritize Motor and Health in Phase 1.** These lines show the highest accident frequency and broadest demand across the target regions.

4. **MIG's Mediterranean positioning can support entry** in Provence-Alpes-Cote d'Azur, Corsica, and Southern Italy, where cultural and climatic similarities may improve brand relevance. Validate this with broker interviews and brand-awareness checks before launch.

5. **Natural disaster risk creates differentiation potential.** Regions like Veneto (score 7/10) and Campania (score 8/10) have elevated climate risk, creating demand for specialized property products where MIG's Southern European expertise is relevant.

---

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Commission detailed feasibility studies** for Ile-de-France (Motor) and Lombardy (Motor + Health) as Phase 1 markets (Q2 2026).

2. **Explore partnership or acquisition targets** in Italy and France to accelerate market entry rather than building from scratch.

3. **Develop a Provence-Alpes-Cote d'Azur pilot programme** leveraging MIG's existing Southern European brand and the region's Mediterranean identity.

4. **Create product adaptations** for the Italian and French regulatory environments (IVASS and ACPR requirements).

5. **Present this analysis to the Board** with a three-year expansion roadmap and capital allocation proposal for the top 5 markets.

---

## Caveats and Limitations

- Market share data reflects MIG's own book and may underestimate total market size in regions where MIG has minimal presence
- Opportunity scoring weights are based on strategic judgment; sensitivity analysis with alternative weightings is recommended
- Regulatory barriers, competitive intensity, and distribution channel availability are not captured in this quantitative model
- Population and economic projections are based on 2025 baseline data and may shift
- This analysis does not account for capital requirements under Solvency II for cross-border expansion
