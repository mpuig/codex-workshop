# Mediterranean Insurance Group (MIG)

## Company Overview

**Mediterranean Insurance Group S.A.** (MIG) is a mid-size European insurance company headquartered in Barcelona, Spain. Founded in 1987 by the Vidal family as a regional motor insurer in Catalonia, MIG has grown over nearly four decades into a multi-line insurer operating across the western Mediterranean basin.

## Strategic Context

### Digital Transformation (Project "Tramuntana")

MIG launched its flagship digital transformation program, **Project Tramuntana**, in 2022 under CEO Elena Vidal's leadership. The program has a five-year roadmap (2022-2027) with a total investment budget of EUR 95 million.

**Current state of technology:**
- **Core policy administration:** SAP for Financial Services (implemented 2006, heavily customized). Stable but inflexible.
- **Claims management:** A mix of a legacy IBM AS/400-based system (motor and home, dating from the 1990s) and Guidewire ClaimCenter (commercial property, partially deployed in 2023).
- **Underwriting:** Largely manual, supported by Excel-based rating tools and a partially implemented Earnix pricing engine (motor only).
- **Data infrastructure:** On-premise Oracle data warehouse with inconsistent data quality across geographies. A Snowflake cloud data platform pilot began in Q3 2024.
- **Customer-facing digital:** A mobile app launched in 2023 (Spain only) with 185.000 downloads but low engagement (12% monthly active users).

**Key Tramuntana workstreams:**
1. **Claims Automation** -- Deploying AI-assisted triage and straight-through processing for low-complexity motor and home claims. Target: 40% of claims auto-adjudicated by 2027.
2. **Underwriting Modernization** -- Migrating from spreadsheet-based rating to a centralized pricing and underwriting platform with real-time data feeds.
3. **Data & Analytics Platform** -- Building a unified cloud data platform (Snowflake + dbt) to enable self-service analytics across all business lines and geographies.
4. **Digital Distribution** -- Expanding direct-to-consumer digital channels and providing agents with modern quoting and servicing tools.

### LusoProtect Acquisition

In March 2024, MIG completed the acquisition of **LusoProtect Seguros S.A.**, a Lisbon-based insurer with EUR 95 million in GWP, primarily in motor and home insurance in Portugal. The acquisition price was EUR 142 million (1.5x book value).

**Integration challenges:**
- LusoProtect runs on a completely separate technology stack (a Portuguese legacy system called SiGeSeg).
- Overlapping agent networks in Lisbon and Porto causing channel conflict.
- Different product wordings and policy terms requiring harmonization.
- Cultural integration: LusoProtect had a more hierarchical, traditional management culture.
- Target: full operational integration by Q2 2026 (currently behind schedule by approximately 4 months).
- Estimated annual synergies of EUR 12 million, of which EUR 4,5 million have been realized as of Q4 2025.

### Key Strategic Challenges

1. **Urban Penetration Gap:** MIG has historically been strongest in rural and suburban markets (particularly in Spain's interior regions and Southern France). It has struggled to compete in major metropolitan areas (Madrid, Barcelona city center, Milan, Lisbon) where digital-native competitors and large multinationals dominate. Urban market share is estimated at 3,2% vs. 8,7% in rural/suburban areas.

2. **Claims Processing Efficiency:** The average motor claim takes 14,3 days to resolve (industry benchmark: 9,5 days). Home claims average 21,7 days (benchmark: 15 days). This is the single largest driver of customer dissatisfaction.

3. **Talent and Skills Gap:** The company struggles to attract technology talent in Barcelona's competitive job market. Average age of IT staff is 48. Only 12% of employees have data literacy training.

4. **Climate Risk Exposure:** With heavy concentration in Mediterranean geographies, MIG faces growing exposure to wildfire, flood, and heatwave-related claims. Nat-cat losses have increased 35% over the past five years.

5. **Regulatory Pressure:** Solvency II reporting requirements continue to increase in complexity. IFRS 17 implementation (completed January 2024) consumed significant actuarial and finance resources.

## Company Culture and Values

MIG positions itself as "the insurer that knows the Mediterranean." Its brand emphasizes:
- **Proximity:** Deep roots in local communities, especially in smaller cities and towns.
- **Trust:** High agent loyalty and long-standing customer relationships (average policy tenure: 7,2 years).
- **Simplicity:** Plain-language policies, transparent pricing.
- **Sustainability:** Committed to net-zero operations by 2035; launched a green buildings discount program in 2024.

The company culture is described internally as "family-oriented but evolving." The Vidal family retains a 28% ownership stake and two board seats. There is a recognized tension between the traditional, relationship-driven culture and the data-driven, agile ways of working that Project Tramuntana demands.
