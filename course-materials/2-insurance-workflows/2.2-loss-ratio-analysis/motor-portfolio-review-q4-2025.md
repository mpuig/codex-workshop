# MIG Motor Portfolio Review -- Q4 2025

**Prepared for:** Chief Underwriting Officer
**Date:** 15/12/2025
**Classification:** Internal -- Management

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **Portfolio loss ratio deteriorated to 71,5% in 2025**, up from 68,6% in 2023 -- a 2,9% increase over two years. The estimated combined ratio of 99,5% means the motor book is now loss-making.
- **Andalusia is in critical territory at 91,1% loss ratio**, driven by high theft frequency and elevated third-party liability costs. This region alone accounts for EUR 73,2M in incurred claims.
- **Valencia has crossed the concerning threshold at 83,1%**, with weather damage (DANA-related flooding) as the primary driver. The trend is worsening quarter on quarter.
- **Immediate pricing and portfolio actions are required** in both regions to prevent further erosion. Without intervention, the portfolio loss ratio is projected to exceed 75% by 2026.

## 4. Key Concerns (Ranked by Impact)

### Concern 1: Andalusia Combined Ratio Exceeds 100%
The region's loss ratio of 91,1% implies a combined ratio of approximately 119,1%. This is the single largest drag on portfolio profitability, responsible for an estimated EUR 16,9M in excess incurred claims above the 70% target.

### Concern 2: Valencia Weather Exposure Is Structural
Valencia's weather damage claims represent 13,3% of the region's incurred costs. Unlike theft (which can be mitigated), weather exposure is increasing due to climate trends in the Mediterranean basin. Current pricing does not reflect this elevated nat-cat risk.

### Concern 3: Commercial Fleet Segment Underperforming Across All Regions
At 74,0% portfolio-wide, the Commercial Fleet segment is the worst-performing product line. This is compounded in problem regions: Andalusia Commercial Fleet runs at 95,0% and Valencia Commercial Fleet at 87,0%.

## 6. Recommendations

### 6,1 Pricing Actions (Implement by Q1 2026)
- **Andalusia: 18-22% rate increase** across all segments. Current loss ratio of 91,1% requires aggressive correction. A 20% increase would bring the projected loss ratio to ~76%, still above target but on a viable path.
- **Valencia: 12-15% rate increase**, with an additional 5% loading for weather-exposed postal codes (coastal zones and river basins).
- **Commercial Fleet nationwide: 8-10% rate increase** to address the segment's structural underperformance.

### 6,2 Portfolio Actions (Implement by Q1 2026)
- **Restrict new Commercial Fleet business in Andalusia** until loss ratio drops below 80%. Current 95,0% is not sustainable.
- **Introduce a minimum premium floor** for Motorcycle policies in Andalusia and Valencia.
- **Review and tighten underwriting guidelines** for fleets with more than 20 vehicles in both regions.

### 6,3 Claims Management Actions (Immediate)
- **Deploy anti-fraud analytics** in Andalusia, focusing on theft claims. The 20% theft share (vs. 12% portfolio average) warrants investigation.
- **Establish a large loss protocol** requiring management sign-off on reserves above EUR 500.000.
- **Accelerate claims settlement** in Valencia for weather-related claims to reduce reserve uncertainty.

### 6,4 Risk Selection Improvements (Q1-Q2 2026)
- **Implement telematics pricing** for Private Motor in Andalusia and Valencia to improve risk segmentation.
- **Enhance flood risk scoring** for Valencia using updated DANA exposure maps from AEMET.
- **Review broker performance** in both regions -- identify whether specific distribution channels are driving adverse selection.

---

## 7. Outlook

### If We Act (Implement Recommendations)
- Projected 2026 portfolio loss ratio: **67-69%** (combined ratio ~95-97%)
- Andalusia projected: **74-78%** (significant improvement but still above target)
- Valencia projected: **70-73%** (returning to acceptable range)
- Expected premium impact: EUR 15-20M reduction from policy non-renewals, offset by rate increases

### If We Don't Act
- Projected 2026 portfolio loss ratio: **73-76%** (combined ratio ~101-104%)
- Andalusia projected: **95-100%+** (further deterioration likely)
- Valencia projected: **85-90%** (continued weather exposure without pricing correction)
- The motor book would become loss-making at portfolio level for the first time since 2019

---

## Key Takeaways

1. The motor portfolio requires urgent corrective action in two regions -- Andalusia and Valencia -- which together represent 30% of premium but are generating disproportionate losses.
2. The root causes are identifiable and addressable: theft in Andalusia, weather exposure in Valencia, and inadequate pricing in both.
3. Commercial Fleet is a problem segment across the board and needs a dedicated pricing review.
4. Without intervention, the motor book will breach 100% combined ratio in 2026.
