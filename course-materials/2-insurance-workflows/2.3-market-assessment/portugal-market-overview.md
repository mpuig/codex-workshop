### Growth Trajectory

Portugal is the fastest-growing insurance market in MIG's footprint, with total market growth of approximately 5,1% in 2025. Key growth drivers include:

- **Economic recovery:** Portugal's GDP growth has been above the EU average, supporting insurance demand.
- **Rising risk awareness:** Post-pandemic health awareness and climate-related property concerns are driving uptake.
- **Vehicle sales rebound:** New vehicle registrations increased 29,9% during January-October 2023, directly boosting motor insurance volumes.
- **Digital adoption:** Growing acceptance of online insurance distribution is expanding market reach.

## 3. Regulatory Environment

### The Regulator: ASF

The **Autoridade de Supervisao de Seguros e Fundos de Pensoes (ASF)** is Portugal's insurance and pension funds supervisory authority. ASF is a member of EIOPA and operates under the EU Solvency II framework.

### Key Regulatory Developments (2024-2026)

| Development | Effective Date | Impact |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act) | January 2025 | Mandatory ICT risk management frameworks, incident reporting, and digital resilience testing for all insurers |
| ASF Regulatory Standard No. 10/2024-R | November 2024 | Enhanced anti-money laundering and counter-terrorist financing measures |
| Cyber incident reporting obligations | April 2025 | Systematic reporting of cyber risks and incidents to ASF |
| Health insurance recommendations | April 2025 | Standardised terms and disclosure requirements for health insurance products |
| Gender diversity recommendations | December 2025 | Board-level diversity targets for insurance undertakings |
| Solvency II Directive 2025/2 updates | 2025-2026 | Enhanced proportionality measures, macroprudential tools, sustainability disclosures |

Sources: [Chambers and Partners -- Insurance & Reinsurance 2026 Portugal](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/insurance-reinsurance-2026/portugal), [DLA Piper -- DORA round-up](https://www.dlapiper.com/en/insights/publications/2025/01/dora-round-up-january-2025)

### Key Regulatory Considerations for MIG

- Portuguese law **prohibits simultaneous life and non-life insurance** by the same entity (with limited exceptions for accident/sickness).
- **Minimum capital requirements** range from EUR 2,5 million to EUR 15 million depending on the class of business.
- ASF has increased supervisory attention on MIG/LusoProtect following the acquisition, given the designation as a "significant insurer" in the Portuguese market.
- Non-life risks are classified as "mass-risk" or "large-risk," with stricter consumer protections for mass-risk (retail) products.

### Key Observations by Line

- **Health dominates:** Unlike most European markets, health/accident is the largest non-life segment in Portugal, driven by gaps in public healthcare coverage (63% public coverage vs. 76% OECD average).
- **Motor is stable:** The second-largest segment is benefiting from strong vehicle sales and EV adoption incentives.
- **Liability is growing fast:** Workers' compensation (85,6% of liability premiums) is a mandatory cover with steady growth.
- **Property is underrepresented:** Property insurance penetration in Portugal remains below the EU average, representing an opportunity.

### Bancassurance Detail

Bancassurance is particularly important in Portugal's insurance market:
- Major partnerships: **Ageas Portugal with Millennium BCP**, Fidelidade with CGD (Caixa Geral de Depositos)
- Banks leverage extensive branch networks and existing customer relationships
- Strongest in life, home (mortgage-linked), and personal accident products
- Exclusivity deals create significant barriers for new entrants

## 6. Additional Market Intelligence

### Motor Insurance Penetration

| Market | Motor Penetration | Avg Premium (estimated) | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|-------|
| Portugal (urban) | ~97% | ~EUR 350-400 | Mandatory; high compliance in Lisbon/Porto |
| Portugal (rural) | ~93% | ~EUR 280-320 | Some enforcement gaps in interior regions |
| Spain (urban) | ~98% | ~EUR 400-450 | Mature market; price comparison site influence |
| EU average | ~98% | ~EUR 450-500 | Higher premiums reflect higher average claims costs |

### Underserved Segments

1. **Cyber insurance for SMEs:** Penetration is extremely low despite growing threats. The cyber insurance segment is projected to grow at 12,8% CAGR.
2. **Commercial property for SMEs:** Many Portuguese SMEs operate without adequate insurance coverage, especially in rural areas (32% penetration vs. 62% in Lisbon/Porto).
3. **Specialty lines:** Construction, renewable energy, and marine cargo insurance have limited dedicated providers in Portugal.
4. **Digital health:** Growing demand for telemedicine and digital health solutions, but few insurers offer integrated products.

### Market Trends

- **Hardening market:** Reinsurance costs for Mediterranean natural catastrophe risks have increased 25-35% over the past three years, putting upward pressure on commercial and property lines.
- **Climate risk:** Wildfire and flood exposure is a growing concern, with some insurers withdrawing from high-risk zones.
- **Consolidation:** Continued market concentration among leading groups, with portfolio transfers and brand rationalisation.
- **DORA compliance:** Significant technology investment required across the sector, creating opportunity for technology-forward entrants.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Portugal is a growing market** with 6,9% CAGR projected through 2028 -- the fastest in MIG's footprint.
2. **The market is highly concentrated** -- Fidelidade alone holds ~30% share, and the top 3 control ~59%.
3. **Health/accident is the dominant non-life line** (36,6%), reflecting public healthcare gaps unique to Portugal.
4. **Bancassurance is critical** -- any growth strategy must account for the banking channel's significant weight.
5. **Significant underserved segments exist** -- particularly cyber, SME commercial, and specialty lines.
6. **Regulatory intensity is increasing** -- DORA, enhanced ASF supervision, and Solvency II updates require compliance investment.
7. **Digital adoption is accelerating** -- a growing insurtech ecosystem signals opportunity for digitally-enabled entrants.
