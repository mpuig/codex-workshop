# Strategy Brief: MIG Portugal Expansion

> **Prepared for:** MIG Executive Committee
> **Prepared by:** Strategy & Corporate Development
> **Date:** February 2026
> **Classification:** Confidential -- Board Material

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**Mediterranean Insurance Group can scale in Portugal with disciplined execution.** The 2024 acquisition of LusoProtect gives MIG an operating platform in the fastest-growing insurance market in its footprint (6,9% CAGR vs. 4,2% in Spain). The Portuguese non-life market is projected to grow from EUR 6,6 billion to EUR 9,2 billion by 2028. MIG's target is to raise share through focused execution in selected segments.

**Our top three opportunities are:**
1. **SME commercial property** -- an underserved segment where MIG's existing Spanish expertise transfers directly, with limited entrenched competition.
2. **Motor expansion** -- improving LusoProtect's existing 6,7% market share through pricing modernisation, fleet products, and superior claims handling.
3. **Residential property cross-sell** -- leveraging the motor customer base and low Portuguese home insurance penetration (42% urban, 19% rural).

**Recommended sequencing:** In Year 1, stabilise the motor book and launch SME commercial packages. In Year 2, scale residential cross-sell and enter group health. In Year 3, evaluate marine cargo and specialty lines based on Year 1 and Year 2 results.

**Key risks:** Integration execution (LusoProtect's legacy SiGeSeg system), competitive response from Fidelidade (~30% market share), and climate risk concentration in the Portuguese property portfolio.

**Financial target:** Grow Portugal GWP from EUR 95M (FY 2025) to EUR 175-200M by FY 2028, achieving a combined ratio below 97% by Year 3.

## 2. Market Context

### Market Size and Dynamics

The Portuguese insurance market generated approximately EUR 14,3 billion in total premiums in 2024, of which non-life accounts for approximately EUR 7,0-7,2 billion. Portugal is the smallest market in MIG's four-country footprint but offers the strongest growth trajectory.

| Metric | Portugal | Spain | EU Average |
|--------|----------|-------|------------|
| Non-life GWP (2024 est.) | ~EUR 7,2B | EUR 40,5B | -- |
| Non-life growth rate | +6,4% | +4,2% | +3,1% |
| Insurance penetration (% GDP) | 5,6% | 4,9% | ~7,5% |
| Digital channel share | ~10% | ~15% | ~20% |
| Home insurance penetration (urban) | 42% | 52% | ~70% |

The penetration gap relative to the EU average represents both untapped demand and MIG's opportunity.

### Competitive Landscape

The market is dominated by Fidelidade (~30% non-life share), followed by Ageas Portugal (~16%) and Generali/Tranquilidade (~11%). The top 3 control approximately 59% of premiums and the top 10 control approximately 89%. For MIG, this means direct price competition in personal lines is unattractive and segment selection is critical.

However, competitive intensity varies significantly by segment. While personal motor is fiercely contested, commercial lines -- particularly SME-focused products -- have meaningfully lower competitive pressure. No single insurer dominates the SME segment, and many small businesses remain uninsured or underinsured.

### Regulatory Landscape

The Autoridade de Supervisao de Seguros e Fundos de Pensoes (ASF) regulates the Portuguese insurance market under the EU Solvency II framework. Key developments include:
- **DORA compliance** (effective January 2025): Mandatory ICT risk management and incident reporting.
- **Enhanced ASF supervision** of MIG/LusoProtect as a "significant insurer" post-acquisition.
- **Prohibition on composite insurance**: Life and non-life must be written by separate entities in Portugal.

## 3. Strategic Rationale

### Why Portugal, Why Now

1. **Growth differential:** Portugal's non-life market is growing at 6,9% CAGR vs. 4,2% in Spain and 3,1% in Italy. Expanding here allows MIG to maintain group-level growth above peer averages.

2. **Acquisition platform:** LusoProtect provides an operating license, an established agent network (~180 agents), a customer base (motor and basic property), and regulatory standing. Building this from scratch would take 2-3 years and cost significantly more.

3. **Capability transfer:** MIG has proven capabilities in SME commercial underwriting (EUR 216M GWP in Spain), health insurance (EUR 270M GWP, +8,7% growth), and digital distribution (Project Tramuntana) that are directly transferable to the Portuguese market.

4. **Reinsurance leverage:** The combined MIG+LusoProtect portfolio (EUR 1,8B+ gross written premium, GWP) improves negotiating power versus standalone LusoProtect placement.

5. **Strategic coherence:** Portugal completes MIG's Iberian footprint, strengthening the "Mediterranean insurer" positioning and enabling cross-border products for Spanish-Portuguese businesses.

### LusoProtect as a Platform: Strengths and Gaps

| Strengths | Gaps |
|-----------|------|
| Licensed and ASF-compliant | Legacy SiGeSeg technology (no API, no digital capability) |
| 6,7% motor market share | No commercial lines underwriting capability |
| ~180 agent network in Lisbon/Porto | Minimal presence outside Lisbon and Porto |
| BPI bancassurance partnership (home) | No health insurance products |
| Profitable commercial book (tourism/hospitality, EUR 18M) | Limited pricing sophistication (no GLM/Earnix) |
| Local management with market knowledge | Cultural integration incomplete; hierarchical structure |

### Synergies

| Category | Annual Synergy (estimated) | Status |
|----------|---------------------------|--------|
| Reinsurance purchasing power | EUR 3-4M | EUR 1,5M realised as of Q4 2025 |
| Claims management efficiency | EUR 2-3M | In progress (Guidewire deployment planned) |

## 4. Recommended Product Strategy

### Sequencing

| Priority | Product Line | Year 1 (2026) | Year 2 (2027) | Year 3 (2028) |
|----------|-------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| **PRIMARY** | Motor (expand) | Deploy Earnix pricing; launch fleet product; mobile claims first notice of loss (FNOL) | Scale fleet segment; usage-based insurance (UBI) pilot | Full digital motor offering |
| **PRIMARY** | Commercial Property (SME) | Launch standardised SME package (property + liability + business interruption, BI) | Expand to mid-market accounts (EUR 5-25M turnover) | Specialty sub-segments (construction, renewable energy) |
| **PRIMARY** | Residential Property | Develop product; recruit underwriters | Launch homeowner + renter via agents and digital; cross-sell from motor | Scale; launch landlord product |
| **SECONDARY** | Health / Group Health | Market research; provider network assessment | Launch group health for corporate clients (25+ lives) | Individual health launch (if group health profitable) |
| **SECONDARY** | Marine Cargo | Feasibility study using MIG Barcelona expertise | Pilot with Portuguese importers/exporters | Scale if profitable |
| **MONITOR** | Cyber Insurance | Track market development; build underwriting capability | Assess market readiness | Launch if penetration reaches critical mass |

### Year 1 Go-to-Market: Key Actions

**Motor:**
- Migrate LusoProtect's motor pricing to MIG's Earnix platform (Q2-Q3 2026).
- Launch fleet product targeting logistics, delivery, and ride-hailing companies.
- Deploy mobile-first claims first notice of loss (FNOL) through LusoProtect's app (reduce average claims time from 14,3 to below 11 days).
- Target: retain 90%+ of existing motor customers; win 2.000 new fleet vehicles.

**Commercial Property (SME):**
- Adapt MIG Spain's "SME Package" (property + liability + business interruption, BI) for Portuguese regulation and market.
- Recruit 3-5 experienced commercial underwriters from the Portuguese market.
- Target sectors: hospitality/tourism (LusoProtect's existing niche), retail, construction, professional services.
- Distribute through brokers (primary) and LusoProtect's agent network (secondary).
- Target: EUR 8-12M new commercial GWP in Year 1.

## 5. Competitive Positioning

### Differentiation Strategy

MIG/LusoProtect should position as **"the insurer for Portuguese businesses"**: a mid-size insurer that combines local knowledge with the scale and expertise of a multi-country group. This positioning avoids head-on competition with Fidelidade in personal lines and focuses resources on underserved commercial segments.

| Positioning Element | How We Differentiate |
|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **For SMEs** | Faster quote turnaround (target: 2 days vs. market average 4-5 days); standardised but flexible packages; digital self-service portal for policy management |
| **For motor customers** | Superior claims experience (faster resolution, mobile FNOL); multi-product discounts; fleet expertise for business customers |
| **For agents/brokers** | Modern quoting tools; responsive underwriting team; cross-border capability for Spanish-Portuguese businesses |
| **Against Fidelidade** | We do not compete on price in personal lines. We compete on service speed, commercial expertise, and digital convenience in segments where Fidelidade is slow to innovate. |
| **Against Allianz/Zurich** | We are more accessible for SMEs. They focus on mid-market and corporate. We bridge the gap between micro-businesses and large corporates. |

### Target Customer Segments

| Segment | Size (estimated) | Priority | Why |
|---------|-----------------|----------|-----|
| SMEs (turnover below EUR 5M) | ~350.000 businesses | Primary | Underinsured; receptive to bundled products; limited incumbent focus |
| Fleet operators (5-50 vehicles) | ~15.000 companies | Primary | Growing segment (delivery, logistics); MIG fleet expertise |
| Urban homeowners (non-mortgage) | ~800.000 households | Secondary | Low penetration; digital-accessible; cross-sell from motor |
| Renters (Lisbon, Porto) | ~400.000 households | Secondary | Very low penetration; young, digital-first demographic |
| Portuguese-Spanish businesses | ~5.000 companies | Niche | Cross-border expertise is unique to MIG |

### Key Assumptions

- Motor retention remains above 87% through the transition period.
- Commercial property loss ratio achievable at 62-64% (consistent with MIG Spain's 63,9%).
- 15-20 new commercial underwriting staff recruited over 3 years.
- SiGeSeg migration to MIG's core platform completed by Q4 2027.
- No major natural catastrophe events exceeding 1-in-50-year severity.
- Portuguese GDP growth remains positive (above 1,5% annually).
- Reinsurance costs do not increase by more than 10% per annum.

### Breakeven Analysis

- **Year 1:** Near-breakeven on underwriting; investment costs produce net loss of EUR ~8,6M.
- **Year 2:** Improved underwriting result; net loss narrows to EUR ~6,6M (cumulative: EUR ~15,2M).
- **Year 3:** Underwriting profit of EUR 6,9M offsets reduced investment spend; net position approximately EUR +0,9M.
- **Cumulative breakeven:** Expected in **Year 3** (2028), with cumulative investment of EUR ~25,5M against cumulative underwriting profit of EUR ~11,2M and synergies of EUR ~30-40M over three years.

> **Flag:** These are base-case projections. A conservative scenario (10% lower GWP, 2 points higher combined ratio) would delay breakeven to Year 4. An optimistic scenario (15% higher GWP, 1 point lower combined ratio) would bring breakeven forward to late Year 2.

## 7. Key Risks and Mitigations

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| 1 | **Integration execution failure** -- SiGeSeg migration delays or data quality issues disrupt operations | Medium-High | High | Phase migration (motor first, then commercial); maintain parallel systems during transition; dedicated integration PMO with board-level sponsorship |
| 2 | **Fidelidade competitive response** -- Market leader cuts prices or launches targeted campaigns in segments MIG enters | Medium | High | Avoid head-on competition in personal motor; focus on commercial/SME where Fidelidade is less agile; build switching costs through service quality and multi-product bundles |
| 3 | **Climate risk concentration** -- Wildfire or flood events in Portugal produce outsized losses on growing property portfolio | Medium | High | Conservative property underwriting limits in Year 1; deploy MIG's climate risk modelling; maintain adequate reinsurance (explore Portugal-specific nat-cat treaty); implement wildfire risk surcharges |
| 4 | **Talent acquisition** -- Inability to recruit experienced commercial underwriters and claims professionals in the Portuguese market | Medium | Medium | Partner with Portuguese universities; offer competitive compensation benchmarked to Fidelidade; consider secondments from MIG Spain during ramp-up |
| 5 | **Agent network defection** -- LusoProtect's existing agents leave during integration uncertainty | Low-Medium | Medium | Communicate early and transparently; protect commission structures for 24 months; invest in agent technology tools; performance incentives for retention and cross-sell |
