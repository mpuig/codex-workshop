## Top 3 Recommendations

### 1. Commercial Property -- SME Focus (Score: 31/40)

Commercial property targeting SMEs is the single most attractive opportunity for MIG in Portugal. The Portuguese SME sector is significantly underinsured (32% penetration in rural areas, 62% in urban centres), and no dominant player has captured this segment. Allianz and Zurich focus on mid-market and corporate accounts, leaving a gap for an insurer willing to build accessible, standardised SME packages.

MIG scores highest here because it can transfer proven SME products from Spain (EUR 216M commercial property GWP, 42.000 policies in force), deploy its underwriting expertise, and leverage LusoProtect's agent network for distribution. The key challenge is platform readiness -- LusoProtect's SiGeSeg system does not support commercial underwriting workflows, requiring technology investment.

**Recommended approach:** Launch a standardised SME package (property + liability + business interruption) using MIG's Spanish product as a template, adapted for Portuguese regulation. Target businesses with less than EUR 5M turnover in the hospitality, retail, and construction sectors.

### 2. Motor -- Expand Existing Book (Score: 31/40)

Motor ties with commercial property at 31 points, but for different reasons. LusoProtect already has a meaningful motor position (6,7% market share, EUR ~62M GWP), and the platform is ready. The opportunity is to improve competitiveness through MIG's Earnix pricing engine, reduce claims handling time (currently 14,3 days; target 10 days), and capture the growing fleet segment driven by last-mile delivery companies.

The constraint is competitive intensity: Fidelidade holds ~28% of motor, and the market is price-sensitive. Growth will come from retention improvement and fleet expansion rather than aggressive personal motor acquisition.

**Recommended approach:** Deploy Earnix pricing in Q3 2026, launch a fleet product targeting logistics and delivery companies, and invest in mobile claims FNOL to differentiate on service.

### 3. Residential Property (Score: 29/40)

Residential property is the natural cross-sell from motor, and Portuguese home insurance penetration (42% in urban areas, 19% in rural) is well below the EU average. MIG's existing home insurance capabilities (EUR 360M GWP, 720.000 policies across the group) provide strong transferable expertise.

The bancassurance barrier is real (mortgage-linked home sales go through bank partners), but LusoProtect's existing BPI relationship and agent network can access the non-mortgage segment: existing homeowners, renters (heavily underpenetrated), and landlords.

**Recommended approach:** Launch homeowner and renter products through LusoProtect's agent channel and digital, bundled with motor at an 8% cross-sell discount. Leverage MIG's climate risk expertise for Portuguese wildfire and flood exposure pricing.
